What is DRS?

This page is an abbreviated overview of what DRS is and how it works in Baldurs Gate 3. A detailed explanation of general damage mechanics can be found on the wiki here

DRS Stands for "Damage Rider Sources

Damage in Baldur's Gate 3 (BG3) can be thought of and conceptualized in 2 main categories:

  • Damage Sources: An action or event which creates a damaging event - attacks, damaging spells, and so on
  • Damage Riders: Damage which triggers when a damage source occurs and "Rides" on it - Lightning Charges, Hex, etc

Due to how certain things are coded in BG3 however, certain things don't work intuitively. Some damage riders work simultaneously as Riders and also as Sources, allowing for other damage riders to attach to them. This creates a behavior where damage riders are able to trigger multiple times off of a single damaging event. You may have noticed this behavior on an attack where you saw a lightning charge or some other rider occurring multiple times off of the single attack.
